# Introduction Skeleton

Generic empirical-economics writing scaffold.

## Paragraph 1: Big question and stakes

What is the broad economic question, and why should a general economics audience care?

## Paragraph 2: Specific gap

What does the closest literature already know, and what remains unresolved?

## Paragraph 3: Empirical setting and identification

What variation identifies the parameter? Why is it credible?

## Paragraph 4: Data and measurement

What data are used? What is newly measured or linked?

## Paragraph 5: Main result

State the main effect, units, magnitude, and benchmark.

## Paragraph 6: Mechanisms, heterogeneity, robustness

What explains the result? What checks make the design credible?

## Paragraph 7: Contributions

Separate contributions clearly:

1. Substantive contribution.
2. Identification or measurement contribution.
3. Literature contribution.
